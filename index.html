Projects

Mobile Crowd Sourcing -- Participatory Sensing

Nowadays, there is an increasing demand to provide real-time environmental information such as air quality, noise level, traffic condition, etc. to citizens in urban areas. The proliferation of sensor-equipped smartphones and the mobility of people are making Mobile Crowdsensing (MCS) an effective way to sense and collect information at a low deployment cost. In MCS, instead of deploying static sensors in urban areas, people with mobile devices play the role of mobile sensors to sense the information of their surroundings and the cellular network is used to transfer data for MCS applications. In order to minizie incentive payment for mobile users, we first predicts the coverage probability of each mobile user based on historical records. Then we efficiently compute the joint coverage probability of multiple users as a combined set and selects the near-minimal set of participants.

Analysis about Water Shortage in China

Studies show that 30% of the land in China suffers from lack of water, which is defined as the total annual water withdrawals (municipal, industrial and agricultural) over the total annual available surface water. Based on GDP, water use etc. data, we dirst calculated the supply and demand of water in different areas, based on which we used a score to indicate the water pressure. The brighter the color in the picture left, the higher water pressure that area has.The we want to dig out the reason of the distribution of pressure. Our research questions include what's the most important factor of water shortage? Is urbanization the main cause of environmental deterioration ? What kind of policy can balance economic development and environmental health?

Popfactor

Out of curiosity, we want to know the differences between popular and unpopular users online.To the best of our knowledge, this is the first quantitative study of how user behavior affects user popularity on social media services.In particular, we studied Twitch, which represents an increasingly popular class of video broadcasting services. By connecting streamers’ behavior on social media platforms, we found that many of the predictive features are related to ways that increase the reach of the streamer, including proactively advertising on popular social media networks (e.g., Twitter), archiving past broadcasts so that they can be viewed later on, broadcasting with a predictable schedule, and playing a variety of different games plays a significant role in getting attention. Surprisingly, video content itself is not that predictive. We hope that the suggestions we made from analyzing data can help hopeful streamers grow a larger fanbase and become popular.

Point of Interest recommendation system

With the development of mobile Internet, social network is becoming more and more popular. A special kind of social network—location-based social networks (LBSNs), such as Gowalla, Foursquare, and Wechat have emerged in recent years. Compared with other social networks, location-based social networks (LBSN s) also consider the geographic factors. As the number of POIs increases dramatically in LBSNs, the POI recommendation plays an important role in helping users discover new spots as well as helping the third-parties provide better services. We tried collaborative filtering and a fully connected neural network by encoding users’ behaviors into one-hot feature.However, the result was not satisfying.Finally, we changed to a logistic regression model, which was much faster and more accurate. However, we still need more work in this area, including figuring out a clever way to encode space information and an efficient way to deal with time series data.

Pagespeed Insights related

This is part of my tasks during my internship in Google. We took advantage of nodes to represent various resources on websites. The directed edges between nodes represent the dependencies. We want to get web quality info from the dependency graph. The most popular visualization library for node graphs is D3 gravity graph. However, each node takes several seconds to get its final position becuase the algorithm needs to iterate many times. In order to improve user experience, I gave up D3 and designed a simple algorithm that need not iterate and improve the speed of rendering nodes. Morever, the algorithm avoid overlappings between nodes.
